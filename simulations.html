Projectile Sampling Distributions

Description

This simulation provides an interactive exploration of sampling distributions in the context of projectile motion. Users can adjust parameters for projectile launches including initial velocity, launch angle, mass, air resistance, and gravity to see how these affect trajectories. The key insight is to focus on the distribution of landing locations, maximum height, and time of flight. Instead of simply showing individual projectile trajectories, the core focus is on what outcomes when there's inherent variability in the launch parameters (e.g., slight variations in initial velocity from shot to shot, or small errors in angle).

  • Use concrete models and number lines to represent whole numbers up to the location of objects.
  • Describe a number less than or equal to 10/20 as a sum (or decomposition) of two numbers.
  • Determine the unknown whole number in an implied or explicit addition or subtraction problem relating three whole numbers.
